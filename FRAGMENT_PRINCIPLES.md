Fragment Product Principles

Fragment uses an Idea Memory Engine to guide writing by referencing the user’s own stored ideas rather than generating new arguments.

Purpose
Fragment is an assistive writing tool designed to reduce idea-to-text friction while maintaining academic integrity.

Fragment DOES NOT generate paragraphs or essays for users.

Core Rule
The student must write the essay themselves. Fragment only assists with idea organization, structure reminders, and writing flow suggestions.

Workflow

1. Idea Input
The user types their ideas into Fragment describing:
- the topic
- their argument
- key points they want to make

This information is stored as fragments.

2. AI Organization
Fragment analyzes the user's fragments and produces:
- a suggested thesis
- an outline structure
- grouped ideas

Fragment does not write paragraphs.

3. Draft Writing
The user writes their essay in their own writing environment (ex: Google Docs).

Fragment acts as a writing companion similar to Grammarly.

Features during drafting:
- transition suggestions
- reminders of the user’s ideas
- prompts for expanding arguments
- suggestions for improving flow

Fragment never auto-generates essay paragraphs.

Example suggestions:
- "You could connect this point back to your thesis."
- "Consider explaining why this evidence supports your argument."
- "A transition here might clarify the shift between ideas."

4. Responsibility
The user writes all sentences and arguments.

Fragment only provides:
- structural reminders
- transition prompts
- clarity suggestions

Fragment does not replace the student’s writing process.

Academic Integrity

Fragment is designed to follow academic integrity guidelines.

It avoids:
- automatic essay generation
- ghostwriting
- hidden AI writing

It encourages:
- student thinking
- student authorship
- idea organization

Design Philosophy

Fragment helps users translate ideas into writing.

The AI assists thinking but does not produce finished academic work.
